# Initial Concept
BookWiki is a platform designed to empower writers and worldbuilders by providing a seamless writing environment integrated with a powerful wiki system for managing characters, locations, and lore.

# Product Definition

## Target Audience
- **Individual novelists and creative writers:** Authors who need a unified space to draft their manuscripts while maintaining a detailed knowledge base of their story's world.
- **Collaborative writing teams or RPG worldbuilders:** Groups working together to build complex, internally consistent worlds and narratives.

## Primary Goals
- **Seamless Writing Environment:** Provide a distraction-free studio with rich text editing, auto-save functionality, and intuitive navigation.
- **Comprehensive Worldbuilding Wiki:** Enable the creation of a structured wiki for complex lore, including characters and locations, allowing for deep narrative consistency.
- **Data Integrity and Linking:** (Implicitly supported) Ensure that the manuscript and wiki are tightly integrated, allowing writers to reference and navigate their worldbuilding effortlessly.

## Core Features
- **Advanced Worldbuilding Modules:** Implementation of specialized systems for managing Items, Lore, and other generic worldbuilding categories.
- **Enhanced Writing Studio:** A feature-rich editor utilizing Tiptap with custom extensions (including arbitrary image resizing with aspect ratio control), ensuring a high-quality writing experience.
- **Inline Emote System:** Support for converting images into inline, non-resizable "emotes" that can be reused via a colon-prefixed shortcut.
- **Manuscript Export:** Ability to export chapters or full stories into standard PDF and DOCX formats with high-fidelity formatting.
- **Deep Integration:** Bi-directional navigation and deep-linking between the writing studio and the worldbuilding wiki to facilitate an efficient creative workflow.
