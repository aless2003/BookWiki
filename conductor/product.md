# Initial Concept
BookWiki is a platform designed to empower writers and worldbuilders by providing a seamless writing environment integrated with a powerful wiki system for managing characters, locations, and lore.

# Product Definition

## Target Audience
- **Individual novelists and creative writers:** Authors who need a unified space to draft their manuscripts while maintaining a detailed knowledge base of their story's world.
- **Collaborative writing teams or RPG worldbuilders:** Groups working together to build complex, internally consistent worlds and narratives.

## Primary Goals
- **Seamless Writing Environment:** Provide a distraction-free studio with rich text editing, auto-save functionality, and intuitive navigation.
- **Comprehensive Worldbuilding Wiki:** Enable the creation of a structured wiki for complex lore, including characters, locations, and species, allowing for deep narrative consistency.
- **Data Integrity and Linking:** (Implicitly supported) Ensure that the manuscript and wiki are tightly integrated, allowing writers to reference and navigate their worldbuilding effortlessly.

## Core Features`n- **Standalone Desktop Application:** A self-contained, cross-platform desktop wrapper providing a native app experience without requiring manual environment setup.`n- **Native Installers:** Automated generation of Windows (.msi and .exe) installers for easy local deployment and use.
- **Advanced Worldbuilding Modules:** Implementation of specialized systems for managing Species & Nature, Items, Lore, and other generic worldbuilding categories.
- **Visualized Species Taxonomy:** A dedicated tree view for exploring the hierarchy of species, races, flora, and fauna, providing clarity on evolutionary or ancestral relationships.
- **Enhanced Writing Studio:** A feature-rich editor utilizing Tiptap with custom extensions (including arbitrary image resizing with aspect ratio control), ensuring a high-quality writing experience.
- **Inline Emote System:** Support for converting images into inline, non-resizable "emotes" with a dedicated management interface for renaming and deleting them.
- **Manuscript Export:** Ability to export chapters or full stories into standard PDF and DOCX formats with high-fidelity formatting.
- **Deep Integration:** Bi-directional navigation and deep-linking between the writing studio and the worldbuilding wiki to facilitate an efficient creative workflow.
- **Multi-Window Support:** The ability to pop-out worldbuilding entries or chapters into secondary OS-level windows via middle-click, allowing for parallel referencing and editing across multiple monitors.
- **Data Portability & Backups:** A robust system for exporting entire projects or individual stories into portable `.bwiki` archives, with full import and merge capabilities to ensure data safety and easy project sharing.
- **Global Save Indicators:** Real-time visual feedback via toast notifications and "Unsaved" state indicators across all modules, ensuring users are always aware of their data's sync status.
